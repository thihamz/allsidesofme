CURATORIAL NOTE
All Sides of me is an encounter with material and meaning, exploring aspects of personhood and the complex, ever-evolving biological and digital systems that inform our views of self. We all see the world through individual experience, spatial, social, political and conceptual. We can discover new realities and memories once we acknowledge the importance of varied and diverse engagement, this being a driver in how we navigate our environment. Before technology, measures of pleasurability were aligned with significant non-technological sensory experiences, such as new tastes, smells, visual stimulus and experiences with touch. Looking at the importance of sensory encounters allows for accessible experience, in particular being inclusive of the diversity of different ways to observe.

Being a part of natural and transformative spaces allows for the exploration of interspecies relationships as well, and the collaborations of human and non-human life forms through the absence of language. Most of nature communicates in exchange of scent, and chemical production/ release, creating an alternative sensual landscape to what we perceive through a human centred lens.

Other parts of experience are represented in landscape and place, and how this helps examine the biographies of a person, inclusive of their environmental experiences outside of themselves. When people find themselves reflected within specific communities, places, aesthetics, movements, tastes, and scents, it influences what they see in other objects, and spaces. The interpretations of senses change depending on what surrounds them, and calls into question their underlying associations; these associations merge as part of the spectatorâ€™s past, present and future.

We encourage all visitors to engage with the work presented, through any way that feels comfortable and rewarding, we hope that within this exhibition there is something that feels representative of you, your interests and the plentiful and various sides of yourself.
